Title: Learning Experience with Artificial Intelligent (AI) in K-12: A Case Study of A Middle School AI Exploration Day

Abstract

To collaborate with a middle school at Northeast Ohio, the purposes of this study are to explore 1) the changes of students’ awareness of AI technologies in real-life experience, understanding of knowledge and skills needed for developing AI technologies, and potential risks and ethical considerations of AI technologies after participating in an AI Exploration Day workshop and competition of the AI Problem-based learning (AI-PBL) project, 2) how students’ perception of their technology skills impacts their learning of AI technologies through the workshop, and 3) how students perceive their learning satisfaction and motivation of learning AI technologies through the workshop. The data were collected via the application form of the AI Exploration day workshop, and workshop feedback survey, and score/feedback provided by AI mentors in the AI-PBL project competition. The results indicate that 1) students’ level of AI awareness was raised after completing the AI Exploration Day workshop, 2) students’ original lower level of AI awareness are not significantly correlated with their learning of the AI concepts, motivation of learning AI technology, and learning satisfaction of the workshop, and 3) students were able to successfully identify the potential benefits, risks/challenges, and ethical concerns of applying AI technologies.

Keywords

Artificial Intelligence (AI) in K-12; Problem-based Learning; AI awareness; Motivation of AI Education; Learning Satisfaction
